## 1. Assessment of Your Current Algorithm (Baseline)

Your **Weighted Multi-Factor Scoring Algorithm** is:

* Deterministic
* Transparent
* Easy to reason about
* Appropriate for MVP / early traction

However, it has **three structural ceilings**:

### A. Linear weighting assumes independence

Industry, stage, check size, and geography are **not independent variables** in real investing behavior.

Example:

* A $250k Angel in Film behaves very differently from a $250k Angel in SaaS.
* Geography matters far less at Series B than at Pre-Seed.

Your model cannot express these interactions.

### B. No learning from outcomes

The algorithm does not improve when:

* Investors ignore intros
* Founders convert to meetings
* Capital is deployed

This prevents compounding advantage.

### C. Keyword matching ≠ investment thesis matching

Alias expansion helps, but:

* “Film finance” vs “content IP roll-ups” vs “media rights arbitrage” are fundamentally different theses
* Investors self-describe inconsistently

## 3. Algorithmic Upgrades (Concrete)

### Upgrade 1: Hard Constraints First (Eliminate Noise)

Before scoring, apply **non-negotiable filters**:

* Check size must overlap ≥ X%
* Stage must be within ±1 level
* Regulatory / geography exclusions respected
* Investor status active in last N months

This immediately improves relevance.

---

### Upgrade 2: Semantic Thesis Matching (Major Gain)

Replace (or augment) keyword-based industry matching with **vector embeddings**.

**How**

* Encode:

  * Founder pitch summary
  * Investor investment thesis / past deals
* Use cosine similarity

**Why**

* Captures intent, not labels
* Solves synonym and jargon drift
* Works cross-industry (e.g., Film × Structured Finance)

**Result**
Industry score becomes **Semantic Fit (0–1)** instead of keyword overlap.

Weight impact: replaces most of the 30% Industry weight.

---

### Upgrade 3: Interaction-Aware Scoring (Non-Linear)

Introduce **conditional weights**:

Example rules:

* If Stage ∈ {Pre-Seed, Seed} → Location weight ↑
* If Investor Type = Family Office → Check size flexibility ↑
* If Industry = Film/Media → Track record weight ↑

This can be implemented as:

* Rule-based multipliers (initially)
* Later replaced by learned coefficients

---

### Upgrade 4: Investor Behavior Signal Layer

Add **behavioral priors**:

Signals:

* Avg response rate
* Acceptance rate by stage
* Time-to-meeting
* Past investments in similar profiles

Use this as a **ranking adjustment**, not a filter.

This prevents “zombie investors” from ranking high.

---

### Upgrade 5: Economic Compatibility (Often Ignored)

Add a **Deal Reality Score**:

* Fund size vs target ownership
* Follow-on capacity
* Portfolio construction constraints
* Typical lead vs follow behavior

This is especially critical for:

* Film finance
* Real estate
* Capital-intensive ventures

## 4. Revised Scoring Formula (Example)

```
Final Score =

+ (Investor Behavior Score × 0.10)
+ (Investor Type Logic × 0.05)
+ (Network / Warmth Bonus × 0.05)
```

Then apply **contextual multipliers**:

```
Final Score = Base Score × Context Multiplier × Activity Multiplier
```

This preserves interpretability while adding realism.
